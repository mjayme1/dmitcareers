Web Designer

Overview

What is a Web Designer? Are you interested in creative work that involves design, layout, look and features? Well, you might be interested in becoming a Web Designer. A Web Designer develops and creates websites for a variety of industries or often works as independent contractors. They are incharge of creating the aesthetics of the website. This involves the color and theme of the website, images, buttons, and overlooks the overall user experience. This person will be incharge of maintenance and additions to the website.

Salary

$73,769 per year

Median Salary: $73,769 (for Web Designers and digital interface designers)

Skills Required: Technical Skills

In order to create visually appealing websites, web designers must possess a broad set of core skills. Web designers must layout pages and content using a site map, and determine how users navigate your site via user flows. You must know what users are looking for on your website and create wireframes to sketch out the important components of the page. There are some skills that must be acquired in order to do this, and web designers should work to master them.

  • 1. Using Design Softwares

    This is the most important skill a web designer should have because this is what is used to help create mockups, design logos, and modify images. They need to be familiar with the different design software such as:

    • •Adobe Photoshop
    • •Sketch
    • •Illustrator

    Knowing how to use design tools will assist you in creating a site that fulfils industry standards and meets the needs of the users and clients. Some designers create designs using web applications, but knowing how to use these specific tools will put you miles ahead in terms

  • 2. Visual Design Principles Knowledge

    To become a skilled web designer, you must understand design principles such as typography, web fonts, and colour palettes. Knowing these principles will assist you in creating a user-friendly website design that will also serve as an effective tool for conveying information.

  • 3. Basic Knowledge in Web Development

    A web designer should also be able to code. Even if only for the sake of being able to build online designs and web interfaces that work on the medium, knowledge of languages like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript is essential, even if only for the basics. Although web designers may choose not to create their own HTML/CSS, understanding how markup and CSS function is critical to their success.

    • •CSS

Skills Required: Soft Skills

Aside from expertise in the technical aspects, non-technical skills, such collaboration and communication, and more soft skills are also important. Here are a few:

  1. 1. Time Management

    Time management skills are essential in today's world of tight deadlines. The rapid pace of project revisions necessitates designs that are adaptable and quick to respond to change requests. Web Designers must keep track of their calendars and follow up on project requirements. They must also be extremely efficient in their work.

  2. 2. Communication

    Good communication skills is especially vital if you want to make it as a Web Designer to convey an idea into clear language, so you will be in the same page with your collogues and clients.

  3. 3. Client Management

    You'll have to collaborate with clients or stakeholders to design websites that are appealing and functional, whether you're an employee or a freelancer. You must know how to convey your ideas to those who may not have a technical background, as well as how to manage expectations while yet delivering solutions that exceed their expectations. If you work as a freelance designer, you need have a plan in place to ensure that your income flow and job backlog are both healthy and realistic.

  4. 4. Good Imagination

    In a field like web design, if a person seeks inspiration from a variety of sources, not just on purpose but out of real desire, he or she has a better chance of progressing and lasting longer in their career. Photography, sketching, art, adventure sports, reading, and even travel provide a solid foundation for Good Imagination.

  5. 5. Organized

    It's half the battle won if you're well-organized as  Web Designer. It's easy to become confused when designing your work. It's critical to pay close attention to to feedback and address each individually. Make a to-do list, segrefte photographs, texts, graphics, videos, and audio into different folders, as well as appropriately labelling them. This not only saves time, but also assures that everything is done correctly.

Web Developer

Overview

Are you interested in developing and maintaining websites which are client and customer focused? You may want to consider a career as a Web Developer!

Web Developers are responsible for programming the code that 'tells' a website how to function. They develop 'user friendly' websites, which means they are easy to navigate for those using them. They also build functions within a website — for example, a form to gather customer information, an input box for collecting emails to send newsletters, or a payment gateway to capture payment details.

Web Developers can be divided into two categories. A front-end Web Developer concentrates on the client side of the website to add aesthetic and functionality. Back-end developers are those who specialise in building up the back end of a website.

One type of developer may focus on the client side to add style and functionality to the website itself (front-end developer) while another may focus on setting up the back end of a site (back-end developer). The work is typically very project-focused and involves collaborating with a team of people who help coordinate the client's needs with the end-product.

Work may also include meeting with clients to discuss their website requirements, or how they may improve their existing website. Web Developers construct the layout of a website, creating a visually interesting home page and user-friendly design, and may sometimes write content for the website. After a website is launched, developers ensure the website is working on all web browsers, testing and updating as needed.

You may have to learn specific skills only depending on what type of developer you’re going for. In terms of the languages you will want to learn, it all depends on whether you want to focus on frontend or backend web development. However, all Web Developers should be proficient in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, so start by learning these three. Skills Required: Soft Skills

Below are some of the most important soft skills a Web Developer should have:

  1. 1. Research Skills

    As a Web Developer, you need strong research skills to help you find solutions to problems you might encounter when starting. With this, you can monitor trends, solve problems, learn alternative methods, keep up with changes, and stay relevant in web development.

  2. 2. Interpersonal and communication skills

    You will need to work and collaborate with other Web Developers and programmers, so you need to develop strong interpersonal skills. This will persuade companies that you can collaborate with others to accomplish the company's objectives.

    Even if you choose to work alone, you will need to cooperate with clients, and your interpersonal skills will be necessary. You can communicate better with clients, understand all of their requirements, and establish successful business relationships by enhancing your interpersonal skills.

  3. 3. Approachability and Helpfulness

    Someone will want to ask you something at some point. It could be about an issue or bug they're having, daily tasks, or anything else. If your teammates don’t feel they can approach you, when something goes wrong, they’re unlikely to ask you for help. That could indicate that a minor problem soon evolves into a major one.

    If you're not approachable or helpful, folks will be less likely to assist you if you need it.

  4. 4. Open-mindedness and creativity

    When you have an open and creative mindset, you are willing to accept new ideas from your team, your clients or yours. Even the worst ideas, if you’re willing to consider them before rejecting them, can produce something great.

  5. 1. Time Management

    Knowing how to manage your time is crucial especially when you have a lot on your plate. Managing your time effectively allows you to focus on the most important things and complete them more efficiently. You may use task boards like Trello to manage and prioritize your to-do list, and remove yourself from distractions but do not forbid yourself to take a rest.

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) Specialist

Overview

A search engine optimization specialist (or SEO Specialist) is someone who analyzes a client's website and implements any necessary changes in order for it to be optimized for search engines. Search engine optimization specialists can be a company employee, an individual consultant, or can work for an SEO consulting firm. These specialists understand how to write specific SEO copy and have in-depth knowledge when it comes to keyword search. If a company (big or small) relies on web traffic for their sales or marketing, hiring a search engine optimization specialist is key to having a successful and profitable business.

Salary

$49,521 per year

The average salary for a SEO Specialist is $49,521 per year in Canada.

Skills Required: Technical Skills

Being a great SEO Specialist requires a wide range of skills. The skills listed are necessary for an effective SEO manager, but being familiar with technical aspects, search engine algorithms, keyword research, and link building is especially important, as is the ability to work with tools that improve SEO performance and make an SEO expert's work routine easier. Some of the most important technical skills for an SEO Specialist are:

  1. 1. Google Tools

    Knowledge of Google tools like Google Analytics and Google Search Console gives an SEO Specialist an edge, and are crucial today for any professional who wants to stay on top of the latest SEO trends and track the outcomes and effectiveness of campaigns and operations.

  2. 2. HTML + CSS + JavaScript

    Basic coding knowledge is almost obligatory, so they cannot be overlooked. You should be able to look at a website's source code and determine the essential elements that are relevant to your campaign. You should also be able to make adjustments and swaps quickly and efficiently without disrupting your site.

  3. 3. Keyword Research

    Among other important SEO skills are keyword research, which is a robust SEO strategy when relevant and well done. The power of keyword research lies in better understanding your target market and how they are searching for your content, services, or products.

  4. 4. Link Building
  5. 5. Technical SEO
  6. 6. A/B Testing

Skills Required: Soft Skills

Below are some of the most important soft skills an SEO Specialist should have:

  1. 1. Critical Thinking

    The first and most crucial skill on our list is analysis, which is a broad term that covers a wide range of SEO aspects. As an SEO professional, you must be able to take data about who, what, when, and where, and grasp the "how" and "why" questions that come with it.

  2. 2. Speaking & Writing Ability

    SEO requires not only confidence but the ability to simplify complex ideas and thoughts into concepts that non-SEOs can comprehend and use to make judgments.

  3. 3. Social Skills and Communication

    The value of communication in SEO can’t be understated. You’ll need to communicate proactively with your other team members and/or clients to ensure your directives are carried out.

  4. 4. Fast Adaptability

    SEO also requires adaptability and thick skin. There's always something new to learn, and SEO tactics may constantly be improved. It's also crucial to be a fast learner because you'll be using a variety of platforms and tools to get the job done.
